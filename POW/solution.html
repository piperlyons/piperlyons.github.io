Solution

The first step in solving this problem was writing a variable equation. So to write the variable equation first we write a let statement

let x = amount of pennies at the start

then, go through what she is giving away and what she is keeping, first, she gives half away and ends up with half of the total

½x

And gives 2 more away

½x-2

Halves again

½(½x-2)

Gives away 2 more

½(½x-2)-2

Halves for the last time

½[½(½x-2)-2]

Takes 2 more away

½[½(½x-2)-2]-2

And ends with 2

½[½(½x-2)-2]-2=2

Once we had the variable equation we had to solve it, first by distributing the innermost parenthesis

½[¼x-1-2]-2=2

Then finish distributing

⅛x-½-1-2=2

Combine like terms

⅛x-3½=2

Then we used the property of equality to start to isolate the variable

⅛x=5½

And finished off the isolation

x=5½÷⅛

Now is where we used our knowledge of multiplying and dividing fractions (you can multiply the reciprocal of the divisor in a division problem, and the reciprocal of either monomial in a product to become the divisor)

x=5½•8

Now we made them improper fractions

x=11/2•8/1

Then cross simplified

x=11•4

Finally multiplied

x=44

And we wrote in the unit

44 pennies

And we had found the answer!
